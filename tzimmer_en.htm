Fanpage Hans Zimmer


Hans Zimmer (born 1957) is since a couple of years one of the most sucessful composers of motion picture soundtracks in Hollywood. In the meanwhile he is very well known by his audience with movies like "The Lion King" or "Gladiator". His extraordinary musical achievement has a positive echo from critics as well. For many years he has won awards for his compositions. There hasn't been a year recently, where he hasn't been nominated for an Oscar. He has won this award (up to now) only once for "The Lion King". 

As friends and fans of his music we have assembled a small, incomplete selection. All data available as MP3-files.

    Crimson Tide (1995)

This atmospheric sound is a optimum for the storyline of the movie. That means the music is really part of the movie itself. 
Especially the variations of the main theme is a great success. The music resemble more a symphony than a normal motion picture soundtrack. The character of the soundtrack is absolutely fantastic because of its electronic sound and effects.
In the business there are a few good composers, but hardly somebody like Hans Zimmer who can adapt the music in a movie in this way.

    The Rock (1996)

The music is composed together with N. Glennie-Smith and H. Gregson-Williams. But the  style of the music is typical Hans Zimmer. The sound is predominantly powerfully mixed with  syntheziser effects. You should relish the full range of the basses and the voluminous sound with headphones.

    Broken Arrow (1996)

The soundtrack is orientated to the old classical western movies. A real treat what Hans Zimmer has made with it. He varies the flowing main theme in different ways with forceful changes of rhythm. The sports contest culminate in a deadly fight for both main characters. Hans Zimmer puts this into great music.

    Peacemaker (1997)

This project is, for Hans Zimmer, the first cooperation with SKG (Spielberg, Katzenberg, Geffen).
The score is similar to Crimson Tide. The solid and  mighty sound is partialy mixed with  melancholy music. Once more the soundtrack fits exactly to the action of the movie. Especially the calm parts with Russian and Serbian sound perfectly embedded.
And everybody can hear it again that Hans Zimmer knows his trade.

    Gladiator (2000)

"Hans Zimmer at his best!" There is nothing else to say. It seems Hans Zimmer wants to excel himself. But the forceful sound reveal another side as well. Seldom before do so many beautiful melodies belong to a motion picture soundtrack. He used best classical elements known normally only from Wagner or Mozart.
Together with Lisa Gerrad, she composed some very beautiful parts, he created an absolutely masterpiece of music.
Our assessment: Oscar potential! (Golden Globe, but not the Oscar)
